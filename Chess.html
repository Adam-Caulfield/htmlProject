Known for its deep complexity and mental challenge, chess is played on a square board divided into 64 squares, where two opponents, each commanding 16 pieces, aim to checkmate the opposing king. The game combines elements of tactics and strategy, requiring players to think several moves ahead while anticipating their opponent’s actions. Every piece on the board — from the pawns to the powerful rooks, knights, and queens — has its own unique movement, offering a variety of ways to approach the game.   1. 16 pawns (8 black, 8 white)
  2. 4 knights (2 black, 2 white)
  3. 4 bishops (2 black, 2 white)
  4. 4 rooks (2 black, 2 white)
  5. 2 queens (1 black, 1 white)
  6. 2 kings (1 black, 1 white)
